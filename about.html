About

My name is Michelle Pupuhi. When I first heard my diagnosis, I thought my life was over. I was in the ICU being treated for diabetic ketoacidosis. I couldn’t see past my diagnosis of type 1 diabetes, all I had was fear that I will never live a normal life. It wasn’t until I met with the dietitian that I found a glimmer of hope. She put my fears to rest, reassuring me that I will still be able to eat a normal diet and lead a normal life.


Her careful explanation and teaching of how to count carbs, how to choose low glycemic foods to curb the glucose spikes and explaining how fats can delay the rise in my blood glucose gave me just what I needed; a boost in confidence to take control of my diagnosis. I didn’t expect to leave the appointment ready to pursue a degree in dietetics, but I knew that with the experience I had in controlling my diabetes coupled with an education I could reach others in similar situations. My interest in helping others cope, treat, and manage their diabetes is what drives me to continue my education.


Being diabetic helped me to prioritize my health, pursue a degree in dietetics, and helped me excel in my studies. The discipline and planning I use to control my diabetes are the same discipline and planning that I put into my schoolwork and jobs. Over the past few years I have gained skills to make better decisions, how to control areas in my life more efficiently, and how to manage the different aspects of work, school and life balance.


I believe I will bring a uniqueness to the dietetics field. I plan to work with other diabetics through community outreach as diabetes is very prominent in Hawai’i. My personal understanding of living with diabetes daily will allow me to connect with my clients on a deeper level.
